Research

My research interests include quite a load of things. Among them I think I'm least bad at Japanese formal semantics and generative syntax.
As a formal semanticist, I work on the formal semantics of various discourse phenomena in Japanese, primarily the uses of discourse particles. In particular, I look at Japanese discourse particles from the perspectives of various types of applied mathematics, including (but not limited to) decision theory, Bayesian inference, and monads and the Kleisli triple (category theory).
As a generative syntactician, I am interested in argument/VP/TP ellipses and the linear ordering of discourse-related elements (such as topics and foci) in Japanese, as well as its cross-linguistic implications. I also seek to explain various typological differences from the perspective of syntax-phonology/prosody interface.
I am also interested in biology of our language faculty, that is, how our trait to acquire and use languages evolved in the species.
My non-linguistic interests include category theory, philosophy of language/mind/politics/science, functional programming, neuroscience, evolutionary biology and aesthetics (music).
